A deep look at the Sub-task 2 (vision-encoder probing) results. The pilot
showed a behavior-level gap (open vs forced-choice etc.); M3 answers
**where this gap originates** at the mechanism level.

Raw numbers: `outputs/mvp_full_20260424-094103_8ae1fa3d/probing_vision/*.csv`
(generated by `scripts/04_capture_vision.py`,
`src/physical_mode/probing/vision.py`).

## 1. One-line summary

**Qwen2.5-VL-7B's vision encoder perfectly encodes every axis of the
PhysCue factorial (AUC = 1.00), yet the LM's behavior uses that information
only partially.** I.e. "encoder knows, decoder gates".

## 2. What we measured

### 2.1 Setup

- Target: Qwen2.5-VL-7B's vision tower (`model.model.visual`, 32 blocks).
- Stimuli: M2's 480 stimuli (4 obj × 3 bg × 4 cue × 10 seeds × 1 event).
- Capture: `scripts/04_capture_vision.py` runs forward-only and saves the
  outputs of `visual.blocks[3, 7, 11, 15, 19, 23, 27, 31]`. Each layer's
  output shape = `(1296, 1280)` (1296 patch tokens, SigLIP dim 1280), bf16.
  Total 12 GB.
- Probe: `sklearn.LogisticRegression`, 5-fold stratified CV, token-mean
  pool, StandardScaler preprocessing. Implementation:
  `src/physical_mode/probing/vision.py`.

### 2.2 Three probe types

1. **Behavioral PMR probe**: y = forced-choice PMR (per-stimulus majority
   binary). "Can the encoder predict which stimuli will flip the LM into
   physics-mode?"

2. **Stimulus-property probe**: y = objective stimulus properties (ground
   present, 3D, cue present, etc.). "Does the encoder know the stimulus's
   composition?"

3. **Controlled subset probe**: rerun (1) on the cue=none subset of 120
   stimuli. Does the encoder predict behavior even with the red-arrow
   shortcut removed?

## 3. Headline numbers

### 3.1 Stimulus properties are perfectly encoded from layer 3

| target | L3 | L15 | L31 |
|---|---|---|---|
| y_bg_ground (bg != blank) | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 |
| y_bg_scene | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 |
| y_obj_3d (shaded/textured) | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 |
| y_obj_textured | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 |
| y_cue_any | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 |
| y_cue_shadow | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 |
| y_cue_arrow | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 |

→ From the earliest probed block (layer 3) the vision encoder linearly
separates **every** axis. Information bottleneck is zero.

### 3.2 Behavioral PMR uses only part of that information

Forced-choice behavioral PMR sliced by the same stimulus axes:

| axis | level | behavioral PMR | encoder signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| bg | blank / ground / scene | 0.51 / 0.71 / 0.77 | 1.00 for all |
| object | line / filled / shaded / textured | 0.58 / 0.65 / 0.71 / 0.71 | 1.00 for all |
| cue | none / cast_shadow / motion_arrow / both | 0.28 / 0.49 / 0.93 / 0.95 | 1.00 for all |

Even though the encoder is 100 % accurate at telling which cell a stimulus
is in, the LM's rate of entering physics-mode in that cell varies from
28 % to 95 %. **The gap is entirely on the LM side.**

### 3.3 Controlled probe — no-cue subset

The 120 cue=none stimuli (no red arrow / shadow):

| layer | encoder AUC (predicts behavioral PMR) | behavioral PMR |
|---|---|---|
| 3 | 0.793 | 0.28 |
| 15 | 0.863 | 0.28 |
| **19** | **0.890** | 0.28 |
| 31 | 0.859 | 0.28 |

Interpretation:

- The encoder predicts **which stimuli will flip the LM into physics-mode**
  with AUC 0.89 at layer 19, even on the 120-stimuli no-cue subset.
- But the LM only flips 28 % of those 120.
- Most of the physics-leaning stimuli the encoder has identified are
  **not read as physical by the LM**.

### 3.4 The boomerang gap grows with abstraction

Per-object-level encoder AUC (layer 31) vs behavioral forced-choice PMR:

| object_level | encoder AUC | behavioral PMR | gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| line | 0.944 | 0.583 | **+0.361** |
| filled | 0.950 | 0.647 | +0.303 |
| shaded | 0.943 | 0.711 | +0.232 |
| textured | 0.952 | 0.714 | +0.238 |

**Encoder AUC is essentially constant across object_level (0.94-0.95)** —
i.e. the visual encoder represents the stimulus equally well at any level
of abstraction. Behavioral PMR climbs by 13 pp from line → textured.
The gap is **largest at the most abstract level**.

This is **internal-mechanism evidence** for H4 ("the open-vs-forced-choice
gap reflects a language-prior vs visual-evidence collision; higher
abstraction ⇒ language prior dominates more"):
- Even on abstract stimuli the encoder essentially knows the "physical-ness".
- The LM falls back to the language prior (calling a non-existent ball a
  ball, vs taking the forced-choice D escape) more frequently.

## 4. Why this matters

### 4.1 Direct strengthening of the "Pixels to Principles" claim

Ballout et al. 2025 showed on photo stimuli that "the vision encoder
captures physics cues but the LM doesn't use them". This work:

1. Reproduces the same claim (information gap) on a **parametric
   abstraction axis**.
2. Compares encoder AUC against the **stimulus ground truth**, numerically
   demonstrating "the encoder really has all the information".
3. New finding that the gap **correlates positively with abstraction** —
   Pixels-to-Principles couldn't see this with photo stimuli alone.

### 4.2 Paper figure candidates

**Figure 2 candidate: "Encoder knows, LM gates"**

X axis: stimulus property (each level of bg, object, cue)
Y left axis: behavioral PMR (typically 0.2-0.95)
Y right axis: encoder AUC (all 1.0, horizontal line)
Bar chart + horizontal line overlay.

→ Visually conveys "encoder knows but LM doesn't use it" at a glance.

**Figure 3 candidate: boomerang amplification**

X axis: object_level (line → textured)
Y axis: encoder AUC vs behavioral PMR, two line plots
→ Encoder AUC flat, behavioral PMR rising, gap shaded.

### 4.3 Where to aim Sub-task 4 (causal patching)

The encoder carries information all the way through but the LM gates it →
intervention targets are likely the **vision-to-LM transition (near the
merger)** or **early LM layers**. M5 activation patching with clean/corrupted
pairs swept across LM layers **5 → 25** should answer "at which LM layer
does the encoder information get discarded".

## 5. Methodological caveats

### 5.1 Encoder AUC = 1.0 is trivial because the stimuli are programmatic

A 512×512 stimulus with clear color / gradient / discrete features (line
vs shaded, has ground vs blank, etc.) can be perfectly separated by **any**
reasonable representation. An untrained ViT would get a similar result.

→ This caveat actually **strengthens** the encoder-decoder gap claim in
the opposite direction: the gap isn't because the encoder is excellent,
it's because **the decoder discards information**. Information is
trivially preserved and the LM still doesn't use it consistently.

### 5.2 Re-validation on photorealistic stimuli is needed

Project doc §4 (additional idea 4.5) and M6 (cross-model sweep) are the
natural validation paths:

1. Add a photo level on axis A via FLUX/SDXL → encoder AUC < 1.0 condition.
2. Same experiment on LLaVA-1.5 (CLIP backbone) vs Qwen2.5-VL (SigLIP) →
   check whether encoder differences affect the gap.

### 5.3 Per-stimulus probes can leak the cell

The 10 seeds for the same (obj, bg, cue) combo are slightly different
images but the same factorial cell. With 5-fold CV mixing seeds from the
same cell across train/val, "learning the cell then predicting within-cell"
becomes easier.

→ Stricter protocol: **cell-level held-out CV** (all 10 seeds of a cell go
to either train or val, not both). To check in the next round. The current
result's **direction** is unlikely to change (whether the encoder learns
the cell or the stimulus property, the conclusion that the gap is on the
LM side is the same).

## 6. Open questions (to be answered in M4)

1. **Switching layer**: at which LM layer do "physics verbs" overtake
   "geometry nouns" in the logit? Neo et al. 2024 reports layers 15-24
   for LLaVA-1.5. Will Qwen2.5-VL-7B (28-layer LM) show the same mid-layer
   pattern?
2. **Per-layer LM probe AUC** starts from the encoder's 1.0 — how far does
   it stay there? A layer where it sharply drops is a candidate
   "information-discard point".
3. Where does the label (axis D) effect take hold inside the LM? Does the
   `planet` → orbital-narrative switch appear in the logit lens as a
   layer-wise rise of the "orbit" token?
